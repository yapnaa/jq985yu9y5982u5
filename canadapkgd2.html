Canada

Canada. Influenced by British heritage Canada is a country which is located to the north of the U.S and also blessed with natural bounty. Banff National Park, the Rocky Mountains and Niagara Falls are some of the major attractions in the country which attract a lot of people around the world.
To a large extent, Canada is influenced by the British and French culture which is one of the main reasons that Canadians speak French. Ottawa is the capital city which has made rapid progress in technological sectors over the years.
When it comes to travel, Canada is blessed with some of the most scenic destinations. You can take cross-city road trips, explore the Rocky mountains for camping, visit the Vancouver Island or hike to the Pacific Rim National Forest which is one of the most famous national parks in Canada.

ITINERARY


    Day 1


Montreal


Upon arrival in Montreal, you will be met and transferred to your hotel. Passengers arriving between 8:00 am and 5:00 pm have the option of being dropped off in downtown Montreal, where you will be free to explore Chinatown, Old Montreal, Notre Dame Basilica and many other attractions.

     Comfort Quality Hotel Montreal or similar

    Day 2


Montreal - Quebec - Montreal


Start your morning with a Montreal city tour to Mount Royal where the city is named after. You will then visit the famous St. Joseph’s Oratory (admission not included), regarded as the largest church in Canada. Today’s journey then brings you to Old Quebec City where many historic European style buildings are well kept. Check out the Fortifications of Quebec, Chateau Frontenac, and Quebec’s Parliament Hill. Perhaps also sip a cup of coffee at the sidewalk café to explore local life style. Return to Montreal in the evening.

Admission fees:
St. Joseph's Oratory of Mount Royal : CAD$3.00/person
Montreal Botanical Garden : CAD$20/Adult; CAD$10/Child (5-17)

    Breakfast is included.

     Comfort Quality Hotel or similar

    Day 3


Montreal - Ottawa - Kingston - Toronto


Departing in the early morning, we'll head to Canada’s Capital city, Ottawa. Here we'll enjoy an Ottawa city tour that includes Parliament Hill, the Peace Tower, Centennial Flame, Rideau Canal, Canadian War Museum (admission not included) and Civilization Museum (admission not included). After lunch time, we'll travel to Kingston, which was once the capital city of Canada. You will see Kingston Fortress and the Old Parliament House (now Kingston’s City Hall) from the bus. We'll arrive in Toronto in the evening.

Admission fees:
Thousand Islands Cruise(Canada Side) : $27.00/Adult; $19.00/Child(5-12)
Canadian War Museum : CAD$19.00/Adult; CAD$12.00/Child(3-12)
Canadian Museum of History : CAD$22.00/Adult; CAD$13.00/Child(3-12)

    Breakfast is included

     Quality Comfort Park Inn by Radisson Best Western or similar

    Day 4


Toronto - Niagara Falls


Today our Canada tour will lead us to the world renowned Niagara Falls. Upon arrival, begin your fall experience with an IMAX movie, Niagara: Miracles, Myths & Magic, to learn about the shocking and existing history of Niagara Falls (admission not included). You will then go up the Skylon Tower (admission not included) for a panoramic view of the magnificent Horseshoe Falls, the American Falls and the Bridal Veil Falls. Afterward, we'll head to the Journey Behind the Falls (admission not included), elevator descend 150 ft through bedrock to tunnels, where have access to outdoor observation decks and two portals located directly behind the Falls. A whole new way to experience world’s famous natural wonder. Next, you will have a chance to join a Niagara helicopter tour (admission not included), which combined adventure and spectacular views. After dinner (own expense), you’ll have time to enjoy the fun at Clifton Hill and Falls Illumination.

Activity fees:
Journey Behind the Falls : CAD$22.50/Adult; CAD$15.00/Child(12-)
Skylon Tower+Lunch : CAD$38.00/Adult;CAD$17.00/Child(12-)
Niagara Fall Meal Plan : CAD$42.00/adult; CAD$37.00/Child(12-)
Skywheel : CAD$13.50/Adult; CAD$8.00/Child(12-); CAD$13.50/Senior(65+)
NIAGARA FALLS IMAX : CAD$15.00/Adult; CAD$11.00/Child(12-)
Sky wheel : CAD$15.00/Adult; CAD$8.00/Child(12-)

    Breakfast is included.

     Quality Inn Travelodge or similar

    Day 5


Niagara Fall - Niagara-on-the-Lake - Toronto


You will begin the day by visiting the World’s Largest Free-Flying Indoor Aviary, Bird Kingdom (admission not included). Discover hundreds of tropical birds as you explore the pathways throughout the multi-level rainforest. Explore the ruins of the Night Jungle that is home to curious owls, bats and fascinating creatures. Next, you’ll head to the famous Table Rock Center at the edge of the Horseshoe Falls, where has fabulous lookout points that place you mere yards away from the thundering Horseshoe Falls. Then we'll board the Horn Blower cruise (admission not included) which will bring you as close as possible to the breathtaking flow of water, power and mist that is the magnificent Niagara Falls. After lunch, we'll proceed to Niagara-on-the-Lake, often called the loveliest town in Ontario. It is a well-preserved 19th-century village, has a long and distinguished history. Pay a visit to the Niagara wine region for a taste test of our world famous ice wine and ginsengs farm before heading back to Toronto.

Activity fees:
Hornblower Niagara Cruise : CAD$25/Adult; CAD$15.50/Child(12-)
Birds kingdom : CAD$20.00/Adult;CAD$16.00/Child(12-)

    Breakfast is included.

     Quality Comfort Park Inn by Radisson Best Western or similar

    Day 6


Toronto


On the final day of our Canada tour you can choose between two activities before we transfer you to the airport. Option A: Toronto Day Tour In the early morning we'll explore Downtown Toronto. You will first make photo stops at the Legislative Assembly of Ontario, University of Toronto and Toronto’s Chinatown. You will then have a chance to climb up the CN Tower (admission not included), a signature icon of Toronto's skyline and a symbol of Canada. On the observation deck, enjoy a panoramic view of Lake Ontario and the city of Toronto. If weather permits, we'll take the sightseeing Central Island Cruise (admission not included)across to the Toronto Islands. Have a walk on the islands and enjoy the beautiful view of Toronto skyline. After lunch, you will visit another Toronto landmark, Casa Loma (admission not included). Constructed over 100 years ago, Casa Loma is noble, mystical and exquisite. Casa Loma’s 5 acres of private gardens with all sorts of flowers flourishing, is now the preferred photography attraction in Toronto. Afterwards, we'll transfer to Toronto Airport for your departure flight. Please book flights leaving after 8:00pm. Option B: Outlet Collection at Niagara Day Tour (available from May to Oct) Today we'll visit Outlet Collection at Niagara, Canada’s largest open-air outlet shopping center with a square footage of 520,000. Located in the beautiful Niagara-on-the-lake just off the QEW, the refreshing new outlet concept features 100+ irresistible outlet brands including Tommy Hilfiger Outlet, J. Crew Factory, Nike Factory Store, Coach, Fossil, Bench Factory Store, Lacoste Outlet, The North Face and Marshalls — all at outlet price. At the south end of the property a food court called “The Eatery” is home to many delicious food options — it can’t be missed. Afterwards, we'll transfer to Toronto Airport for your departure flight. Please book flights leaving after 8:00pm.

Activity fees:
CN Tower : $38.00/adult; $28.00/child(4-12); $34.00/Senior (65+)
Casa Loma : $30.00/Adult; $20.00/Child(4-13); Free/Child(3-); $25.00/Senior(65+)
Central Island Cruise : CAD$30.00/Adult; CAD$18.00/Child(12-); CAD$28.00/Senior(65+)
