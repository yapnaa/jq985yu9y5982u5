Canada

Canada. Influenced by British heritage Canada is a country which is located to the north of the U.S and also blessed with natural bounty. Banff National Park, the Rocky Mountains and Niagara Falls are some of the major attractions in the country which attract a lot of people around the world.
To a large extent, Canada is influenced by the British and French culture which is one of the main reasons that Canadians speak French. Ottawa is the capital city which has made rapid progress in technological sectors over the years.
When it comes to travel, Canada is blessed with some of the most scenic destinations. You can take cross-city road trips, explore the Rocky mountains for camping, visit the Vancouver Island or hike to the Pacific Rim National Forest which is one of the most famous national parks in Canada.

    Day 3


New York City - Princeton


Our East Coast tour will begin with a New York tour and a visit to the financial capital of the world, Wall Street, home to the New York Stock Exchange. Along the way, we can also see the Old City Hall and the signature Charging Bull sculpture. Next, we will head to the top of the One World Trade Center (admission not included). Afterwards we'll see the iconic Statue of Liberty with a Liberty Island Cruise (own expense). You'll enjoy magnificent views of the New York skyline as well as Brooklyn Bridge, Ellis Island and a close-up view of Statue of Liberty. Then we'll head to the USS Intrepid (admission not included), a stunning museum of a variety of aircrafts. From here we'll pass through midtown, spotting the United Nation (UN), Fifth Avenue, and the Rockefeller Center, a national historic landmark and one of New York’s most inspiring locations. The last destination for the day will be Times Square - the crossroad of the world. Upon arrival, visitor will also have a chance to explore the Ripley’s Believe It Not (admission is not included)!

    Day 4


Philadelphia - Washington. D.C.


Our tour bus will depart from hotel to Philadelphia, the second capital of the United States. Upon arrival, you will visit the historical Independence Hall where George Washington was appointed Commander-in-Chief of the Continental Army. Right beside the Independence Hall is the Liberty Bell Center, the symbol of Freedom and Liberty for the past 200 years. The bus then will head to Washington D.C. and the first stop there will be the National Museum of Natural History where the famed curse Hope Diamond is located at. Afterward the bus will take us to the Capitol, we will be able to take a photo outside of the US Congress Building where many of United States' laws are passed. Next stop will be the Madame Tussaud's President Wax Museum (admission not included), in here you will be able to take photos with all of the United States' presidents as well as many famous celebrities! Following next will be the White House (South Lawn View), the principal residence and workplace of the U.S. President. Then you will visit one of the most famous landmarks in the world, the Lincoln Memorial. And the final attraction of the day will be the D.C. Harbor Cruise (admission not included) which rests on top of the Potomac River- the national river of the United States, with the exclusive view of The Pentagon, the Department of Defense.

Washington, D.C. - Niagara Falls


The day begins as we travel from the hotel to Corning Museum of Glass (admission not included), it is the largest glass museum in the world. You will have the opportunity to see a live Hot Glass Show inside the museum and learn about the history of glass making. Afterwards we will head to Niagara City and cross the border into Canada. Afterwards we will check into the hotel and guest will have free time to come out and experience the famous Illumination of Niagara Falls (Canada side) and activities at Clifton Hill.

    Day 6


Niagara Falls - Toronto


Today we will enjoy the Legend of Niagara movie in the IMAX Movie Theater (admission not included) following by the world famous Hornblower Niagara Falls cruise (seasonal between April and November, admission not included) that will take you right into the heart of the Niagara Falls to experience the thunderous power of one of the natural wonders of the world. We will then reach to the tallest point of the Niagara City, for the Skylon Tower Lunch (admission not included), while enjoying a spectacular day view of the Niagara Falls. Heading over to Toronto afterwards, once we reach to Toronto harbor we are going right onto the Lake Ontario for the Toronto City Cruise (seasonal between April and November, admission not included), where you can overlook Toronto for the best pictures of the city. When return, we will reach up to the tallest point in the entire western hemisphere, the CN Tower (admission not included) and experience the first glass floor in the world. Following by the Ripley’s Aquarium (admission not included), currently one of the most popular attractions in Canada that features several aquatic exhibits including a walk-through tank. While passing through downtown Toronto, we will be able to see the Toronto City Hall, the landmark of the city. Tour will then visit Toronto Chinatown(Dinner, additional cost) for unique experience of local cultures.

Montreal - Quebec City - Boston


Leave hotel for Quebec City, the oldest city in North America. First stop will be one of the only two cities in the entire North America that is still surrounded by fortified wall, Old Quebec. We will have an Old Town Quebec In-Depth Walking Tour (admission not included), the oldest and most historical part of the Quebec City，the La Citadelle and Cheatau Fronternec (admission not included). Boston/New York


During the morning, we'll travel to nearby Cambridge, Massachusetts to visit Harvard Square, Harvard University and Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
